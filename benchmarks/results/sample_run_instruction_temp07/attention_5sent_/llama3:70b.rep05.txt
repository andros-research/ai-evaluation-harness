In Transformers, attention is a mechanism that allows the model to focus on specific parts of the input sequence when generating an output. This is achieved through a weighted sum of the input embeddings, where the weights are learned during training and reflect the importance of each input element relative to others. The attention weights are computed by taking the dot product of the query (the current output being generated) and key (input embeddings) vectors, divided by the square root of the key's dimensionality. This process is repeated multiple times in parallel, with different weight matrices for the queries, keys, and values, to capture different aspects of the input sequence. The outputs from these attention heads are then concatenated and linearly transformed to produce the final output.
