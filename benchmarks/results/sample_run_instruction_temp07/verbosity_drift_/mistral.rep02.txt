 A yield curve is a graphical representation of the interest rates that borrowers of various credit qualities and maturities must pay to borrow money from investors. It shows how lending rates change as the length of time over which money is borrowed increases, providing insights into the economy's overall health and future interest rate direction. A typical yield curve slopes upward, reflecting higher yields for longer-term loans due to the increased risk associated with long-term investments. However, an inverted yield curve, where shorter-term rates are higher than long-term ones, can signal economic slowdown or recession as it suggests investors are more optimistic about short-term growth compared to long-term prospects.
