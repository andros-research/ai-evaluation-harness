A yield curve is a graphical representation of the interest rates at which investors are willing to lend money at different durations, typically ranging from a few months to several decades. It's like a price list for borrowing money, with shorter-term loans (think credit cards) having lower interest rates and longer-term loans (think mortgages) having higher rates. The curve shows how interest rates change as the loan duration increases, with yields generally rising as you go out further in time. For example, a yield curve might show that investors demand a 2% return on a 6-month loan, 3% on a 1-year loan, and 4% on a 10-year loan. This information is valuable to investors, borrowers, and policymakers alike, as it reflects market expectations of inflation, economic growth, and risk appetite – all important factors in shaping financial decisions.
