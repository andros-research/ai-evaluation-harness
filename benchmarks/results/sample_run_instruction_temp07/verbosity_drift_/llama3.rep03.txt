A yield curve is a graphical representation of the interest rates that lenders charge borrowers across different periods of time, typically ranging from short-term (e.g., 3 months) to long-term (e.g., 30 years). It's like a snapshot of the market's expectations about future borrowing costs. The curve shows how interest rates change as you move out in time: for instance, a yield curve might show that lenders demand a higher return on investments if they're willing to lend money for 10 years versus just 3 months. This is because investors are generally more risk-averse when lending for longer periods, so they require a premium to compensate for the increased uncertainty and potential losses. The shape of the yield curve can also indicate market sentiment and even influence borrowing rates for consumers and businesses.
