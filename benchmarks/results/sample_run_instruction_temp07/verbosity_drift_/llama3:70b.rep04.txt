A yield curve is a graphical representation of the relationship between the interest rates (or yields) offered by bonds of different maturities and their corresponding prices. Essentially, it's a way to visualize how much investors are willing to lend money to a borrower (like a government or company) for varying periods of time. The curve shows that shorter-term loans typically offer lower interest rates, while longer-term loans require higher interest rates to compensate for the increased risk and uncertainty over time. For instance, you might be able to borrow money for a year at 2% interest, but if you want to borrow it for 10 years, you might have to pay 4% interest. The yield curve helps investors and analysts understand the market's expectations of future economic conditions, such as inflation or recession risks, which can influence interest rates and borrowing costs.
