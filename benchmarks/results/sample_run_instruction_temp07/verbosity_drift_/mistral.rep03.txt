 A yield curve is a graphical representation of the interest rates that a borrower (like a government or corporation) must pay on debt obligations with different maturities at a specific point in time. It shows the relationship between the yield (or return on investment) and the length of time until the principal is repaid for bonds issued by the same issuer. Normally, a typical yield curve slopes upward, meaning longer-term bonds have higher yields than shorter-term ones, as investors demand additional compensation for tying up their money for longer periods. However, an inverted yield curve (when short-term bonds have higher yields) can be a signal that a recession may be on the horizon.
