 A yield curve is a graphical representation of the interest rates that borrowers must pay to borrow money for different periods (or maturities) of time. It shows how lenders demand higher returns (yields) for longer-term loans due to the increased uncertainty and risk associated with those investments. A normal yield curve slopes upward, indicating that shorter-term interest rates are lower than long-term rates, reflecting the market's expectation for economic growth. However, an inverted yield curve (where short-term rates are higher than long-term rates) is often seen as a sign of economic slowdown or recession because it suggests investors are seeking safer investments due to pessimism about future economic conditions.
