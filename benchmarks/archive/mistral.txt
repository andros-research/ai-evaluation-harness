 Attention in Transformers is a mechanism that allows the model to focus on relevant parts of the input sequence when producing an output. It's a weighted averaging process that assigns higher weights to important words or phrases and lower weights to less significant ones. The attention mechanism calculates scores for every possible combination of input and output elements, creating alignment pairs. These scores are then used to compute weighted sums of the input sequence to produce an attended output representation. This process helps Transformers to better capture long-range dependencies and improve the model's ability to understand context in text data. Finally, there are different flavors of attention like Scaled Dot Product Attention (SDPA), Multi-Head Self-Attention (MHSA), and Bahdanau, Cho, and Bengio's Attention Mechanism, each with its own unique advantages for processing various types of data.
