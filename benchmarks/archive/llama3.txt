In Transformers, attention is a mechanism that allows the model to focus on specific parts of the input sequence when making predictions. It does this by computing a weighted sum of the input elements, where the weights are learned during training and represent the importance of each element for predicting the output. The attention mechanism is applied at each layer of the Transformer encoder and decoder, allowing it to selectively attend to different parts of the input sequence as needed. This enables the model to capture long-range dependencies and relationships in the input data that may not be apparent through simple sequence processing alone. By attending to relevant parts of the input, the Transformer can generate more accurate and contextually informed output.
